About Busted Tusk Kauri Co.

Busted Tusk is the work of an eccentric traveling artisan woodsmith dedicated to honoring the sacred material of New Zealand kauri — a wood that holds the deep breath of ancient ecosystems and the weight of history. Reclaimed from a 1946 bluewater sailboat built by a master craftsman, the kauri carries whispers of the sea, the forest, and the hands that shaped it.

Each pendant and artifact crafted here is an offering — a tangible connection to the vast web of life, to indigenous Māori heritage, and to the stories buried beneath layers of colonialism and maritime exploration. This work seeks not only to preserve wood but to carry forward the truths of deep ecology, respect for the land, and a humble acknowledgment of the complex histories entangled with every grain.

The History Layer

The kauri is more than timber; it is a living archive. For Māori, these mighty trees are sacred, embodying the spirit of the land and the ancestors. The arrival of British colonial powers brought upheaval, exploitation, and chains — both literal and metaphorical — that echo through history.

Busted Tusk’s reclaimed wood speaks to this layered past: from the voyages of James Cook and Pacific maritime exploration to the resilient cultures that endured beneath colonial shadows. Today, the bohemian spirit of Sausalito’s artisan community continues this tradition of creativity and respect, bridging worlds and histories through craft.
